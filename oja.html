Oja Depth

Definition: The Oja depth of a point x is found by summing the areas of all simplices (generalized triangles) which include x as a vertex. Where the simplices summed are formed by x and other vertices which are in the pointset.

Is this a good depth measure?

Affine Invariance
Yes, the Oja depth measure is affine invariant. This is because under an affine transformation, the volume of the simplices is scaled by a factor dependent on the determinant of the matrix deciding the linear transformation. Because all volumes are scaled equally, the relative depth ranking of points does not change.
Maximality at the Center
The Oja depth function does not necessarily have a unique center, but the depth at any center will be maximal.
Monotonicity
The depth function should be monotone decreasing as points move away from the 'center' of the distribution on some ray.
Vanishing at Infinity
Because, the depth of point is inversely related to the volume of the simplices, points at infinity have depth approaching the minimum of the Oja depth function.
Robustness
In one case, it has been shown that the breakdown point of the Oja depth function is zero.
